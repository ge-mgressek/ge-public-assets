The coconut tree is unlike any other plant on Earth. While a typical tree stores carbon in its trunk over decades, the coconut tree acts like a rapid biological pump, capturing immense amounts of CO₂ and converting it into harvestable biomass multiple times per year.

From Silicon Valley to the Tree of Life

A short story, by Matthew Grecsek, Founder & CEO

How a journey to a remote family coconut plantation inspired a system for planetary-scale carbon removal, natural abundance, and shared prosperity.

In 2010, my life took a turn I never expected. I traded Silicon Valley’s fast-paced world of technology for a family coconut plantation on a small tropical island in the Philippine Sea.

It was here I first met the mystical “Tree of Life.” Imagine 3.6 million hectares covered by 350 million coconut trees, a living ocean of green. The sheer scale and beauty were breathtaking, matched only by the generosity of the people who called it home. For a moment, I thought I had stumbled into a hidden suburb of the Garden of Eden.

A Paradise with a Problem

But paradise had a secret.

As the proud new owner of a coconut farm, I was shocked to learn that the annual harvest from hundreds of trees couldn't even pay for a plane ticket back to San Francisco. For families who had worked these groves for generations, the bounty of a paradise lost was barely enough to survive.

Beneath this vast canopy, subsistence farming persisted unchanged for a thousand years. Donning my Silicon Valley problem-solving glasses, I thought: "This looks like a job for technology." I gave myself six months to modernize our plantation, hoping it could serve as a model for the industry.

A Decade of Discovery

Six months became ten years.

I embedded with farmers, ran experiments, built pilot factories, and navigated the enchanted wilderness; often with no cell service, no internet, and only sometimes electricity. There were typhoons, bureaucratic mazes, and wild journeys to remote islands near the Sulu peninsula for research collaborations.

Along the way, we donated textbooks to schools, organized food programs, and led relief efforts after devastating storms. But we also witnessed nature's fury firsthand. The Philippines endures about 20 typhoons each year, and climate change has made them stronger. When super-typhoon Odette damaged our family home in 2021, it became clear: our mission had to embrace climate resilience and adaptation as much as innovation.

The Breakthrough

The legacy coconut industry was a system out of balance, not solvable with business-school frameworks. To change it, we needed a new paradigm — one inspired by the laws of physics

Our first breakthrough was an advanced off-grid manufacturing platform that could process coconuts in the harsh conditions of tropical rainforests. But each day left us with mountains of empty shells and husks piling into costly waste.

When COVID travel restrictions grounded us in the US, we turned that problem into an opportunity. After years of research, we solved a 20-year-old material science puzzle: turning coconut husks into strong, eco-friendly, binder-free fiberboards.

Together, these two innovations became the foundation of our Coconut Processing Unit (CPU), a system designed to unlock the hidden value in coconut plantations worldwide and create something new and enduring. In 2025, we launched the first commercial-scale CPU.

Looking Forward

Now, as we begin deploying thousands of CPUs around the world, we are proud to stand at the intersection of People, Planet, and Prosperity — restoring farmers' rightful inheritance, renewing the promise of the land for all people, and reimagining what’s possible when human innovation works in harmony with nature.

Mother Nature is a beautiful dance partner when we approach her with respect and appreciation, yielding abundance, naturally.

From Silicon Valley to the Tree of Life, this has been a journey of discovery, resilience, and hope... a grand adventure we are only just beginning.
